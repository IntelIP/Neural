# Neural Comparison Brief

## Purpose

Give prospective users a concise explanation of where Neural fits relative to official exchange SDKs, data vendors, and broad quant platforms.

## The Short Version

Choose Neural if you want the fastest Kalshi-first path from developer setup to prediction-market workflows in code.

## Neural Vs Kalshi Official SDKs

Use Kalshi directly when:

- you want raw first-party coverage
- you are comfortable building directly on exchange primitives
- you do not need an opinionated workflow layer

Use Neural when:

- you want a narrower SDK surface built around developer workflows
- you want paper trading and CLI readiness checks alongside auth and market data
- you want an open-core layer that can grow into premium tooling later

## Neural Vs Polymarket Tooling

Use Polymarket directly when:

- you are focused on Polymarket-native integrations
- you need its first-party trading and market access directly

Use Neural when:

- your main path is still Kalshi-first
- you want Polymarket only as a secondary beta read and streaming surface
- you care more about workflow consistency than full venue coverage today

## Neural Vs Oddpool

Use Oddpool when:

- your main need is cross-venue data, discovery, arbitrage, whale tracking, and market intelligence

Use Neural when:

- your main need is code-level trading workflow infrastructure
- you want auth, market data, paper trading, and selected live flows in one SDK
- you are not buying a cross-venue data product first

## Neural Vs QuantConnect

Use QuantConnect when:

- you want a broad algorithmic-trading platform
- you need deep backtesting, cloud execution, and a mature general-purpose quant stack

Use Neural when:

- you want prediction-market-native tooling instead of a broad multi-asset platform
- you want a smaller surface area and faster setup
- you care about Kalshi-first workflows more than general quant infrastructure

## Current Neural Boundary

Neural is best understood as:

- a Kalshi-first SDK beta
- not a full multi-exchange platform
- not a cross-venue data company
- not a general quant operating system

## Recommended Public Comparison Line

Kalshi gives you the raw exchange API. Oddpool gives you cross-venue market intelligence. QuantConnect gives you a broad quant platform. Neural gives you the narrowest Kalshi-first SDK path from auth and market data to paper trading and selected live workflows.
